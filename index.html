Chayan Kumar Saha

Software developer and Bioinformatician · Bionamic AB · Lund · Sweden

Hi and welcome!

I have a PhD in Molecular Biology (Speciality: Bioinformatics) from Umeå University, Sweden. I am currently employed at Bionamic AB in Lund, Sweden, as a software developer and bioinformatician. I have extensive experience as a bioinformatician and researcher with coding, tool development, and data analysis. Now, at Bionamic, I apply this knowledge to the development of software that simplifies and accelerates antibody development.

My doctoral study was initially focused on the functional evolution of RelA/SpoT Homologue enzymes that produce the alarmone nucleotide (p)ppGpp, which regulates a variety of bacterial physiological functions. I developed a python tool FlaGs (www.webflags.se) that can analyse conservation of gene neighbourhood at long evolutionary distances (PMID: 32956448). Using FlaGs, we have discovered that some homologues of (p)ppGpp synthesising enzymes known as SASs (Small Alarmone Sythetases) can be toxins in a novel toxin-antitoxin system (PMID: 32345719). Recently, we have discovered a hyperpromiscuous antitoxin domain that can neutralise dozens of diverse toxin domains (PMID: 35121656). Additionally, I developed a bioinformatics tool called NetFlax to uncover the core network of proteinaceous toxin and antitoxin systems in all microbes.
